How to make Continuous from Present, Past and Future tenses.

The main thing about converting Present, Past, and Future SIMPLE tenses into Continuous tense is that we just have to use verb "to be" add -ing ending in the end of the main verb. And for the perfect tenses we use have + been + ing form of the main verb.:

The formulas for Continuous tenses are:

For Present Simple Continuous: Subject + to be + Verb(1rd form)-ing + Object

For Past Simple Continuous: Subject + to be (past) + Verb(1rd form)-ing + Object

For Future Simple Continuous: Subject + will + be + Verb(1rd form)-ing + Object

 

For Present Perfect Continuous: Subject + have + been + Verb(1rd form)-ing + Object

For Past Perfect Continuous: Subject + had + been + Verb(1rd form)-ing + Object

For Future Perfect Continuous: Subject + will + have been + Verb(1rd form)-ing + Object

 

So, from the above formulas you can see that we can transform all tenses from base tense to continuous:

In the formula for Present Simple (Subject + Verb (1st form) + _/s(es) + Object) we just add "to be" verb and transform Verb (1st form) into Gerund (-ing).

In the formula of the Present Perfect we just adding "been" and the again transform Verb (1st form) into Gerund (-ing).

 

So here, from all above you can see all the differences between all of the English tenses

Examples:

Present Simple: I run every morning.

Present Simple Continuous: I am running to a park at the moment.

Past Simple: I walked yesterday.

Past Simple Continuous: I was running when she called.

Future Simple: I will run tomorrow morning.

Future Simple Continuous: I will be running tomorrow at 9 A.M.

 

Present Perfect: I have coocked a soup

Present Perfect Continuous: I have been coocking the soup for an hour already.

Past Perfect: I had cooked the soup when already you drank all my cola.

Past Perfect Continuous: I had been cooking your soup while you were drinking my cola.

Future Perfect: I will have cooked your soup before you drink all cola

Future Perfect Continuous: I will have been cooking the soup tomorrow at 11 A.M.
